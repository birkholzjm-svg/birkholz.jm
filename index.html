I am an electrochemical engineer specializing in the development of next-generation energy storage and conversion systems. At VoltStorage in Munich, I led the design and optimization of iron redox flow battery cells and hydrogen-iron hybrid architectures — taking concepts from early-stage prototyping through to validated, performance-tested hardware.

My background combines a Master’s degree in Chemical Engineering from the Technical University of Munich with hands-on prototyping expertise across flow batteries, fuel cells, and electrolysis. I bring a rigorous, data-driven methodology to every project — from cell architecture design and flow field engineering to full electrochemical characterization.
